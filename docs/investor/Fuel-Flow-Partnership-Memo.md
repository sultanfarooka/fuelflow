## 2. Problem & opportunity

Most independent and small-chain pumps in Pakistan still run on paper shift books, informal udhaar, and spreadsheets — causing leakage, disputes, and no real-time view for the owner.

| Pain point | Fuel Flow solves it |
|---|---|
| Manual shift books & meter readings | Shift + nozzle tracking, shortage on nozzleman ledger |
| Udhaar on paper / WhatsApp | Credit ledger with limits and aging |
| Stock vs dip mismatch | Tank inventory + dip charts + variance alerts |
| OGRA price changes | Price history, sales split by rate |
| Multiple stations, no overview | Consolidated owner dashboard |
| **Owner not on-site** | **Owner views live reports and manages stations from his mobile, with instant notifications for shortages, low stock, price changes, and daily summaries** |

Generic accounting software doesn't understand pumps, dip charts, nozzle meters, or shift culture. Fuel Flow is purpose-built for this.

### Glossary

**Udhaar** — credit sale (customer owes station). **Dip reading** — fuel level via dip stick (mm → liters). **OGRA** — sets official fuel prices. **PMG/HSD/HOBC** — petrol / diesel / hi-octane. **Shift** — sales period opened & closed with meter readings. **Shortage** — gap between expected and collected cash, often on nozzleman's ledger.
